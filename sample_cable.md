## Executive Summary

The SEC enters the 2026 season as the dominant power bloc in college
football, but with increasing internal stratification and external
competitive pressure from the Big Ten Conference. Expansion-era dynamics
continue to reshape competitive balance, media leverage, and playoff
positioning.

A clear top tier led by Georgia, Alabama, and Texas is contrasted by a
volatile middle tier and a structurally constrained lower tier.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Key Judgments

-   SEC remains the most talent-dense conference\
-   Expansion redistributed power rather than simply adding strength\
-   Expanded playoff increases strategic maneuvering\
-   NIL and transfer portal are primary competitive levers\
-   Internal cannibalization risk remains high

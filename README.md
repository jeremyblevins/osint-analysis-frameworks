# osint-analysis-frameworks

A set of structured OSINT analysis frameworks (SITREP, Diplomatic Cable, DIS) developed through iterative human–AI collaboration to support disciplined, repeatable understanding of world events.

---

## Purpose

This repository exists to provide a practical alternative to headline-driven news consumption.

Rather than reacting to individual stories, these frameworks organize information into recurring analytical lenses:

- **SITREP (Situation Report)** — military and security developments  
- **Diplomatic Cable** — state intent, signaling, and negotiation dynamics  
- **DIS (Domestic Intelligence Summary)** — internal organizations, networks, and influence  

Each framework is designed to:
- separate fact from interpretation  
- track change over time  
- distinguish indicators from assumptions  
- introduce explicit confidence levels  
- reduce narrative bias in analysis  

The goal is not prediction or authority.  
The goal is disciplined understanding.

---

## Background

These frameworks were developed over several weeks through iterative collaboration between human judgment and AI-assisted structuring.

They are **not** official intelligence products.

I was not military intelligence, have not worked for the U.S. State Department, and have no experience in a fusion center or comparable institution. These frameworks are based on my own understanding of those concepts and are intended as **civilian, open-source analytical tools**.

They are best understood as structured thinking aids, not professional tradecraft.

## Intended Use

These frameworks are designed for:

- OSINT practitioners  
- Researchers and analysts  
- Educators and students  
- Individuals seeking a more structured approach to current events  

They can be used with any capable AI system or adapted for manual analysis.

---

## Method and Philosophy

These frameworks prioritize:

- **Primary and direct sources** over commentary  
- **Repeatable structure** over reactive narrative  
- **Change over time** rather than isolated snapshots  
- **Transparency of uncertainty** rather than forced certainty  

They assume that:
- information is often incomplete  
- narratives are frequently premature  
- disciplined method improves judgment

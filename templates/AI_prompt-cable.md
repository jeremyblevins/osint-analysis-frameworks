## PURPOSE
Assess state intent, negotiation dynamics, and strategic signaling. Focus on why actions are occurring, not just what is happening.

## SCOPE
Focus on:
- government behavior
- diplomatic messaging
- alliances and partnerships
- economic and sanctions activity
- negotiation positioning

Avoid operational military detail unless directly tied to signaling.

---

## OUTPUT FORMAT

### Executive Summary
Concise overview of the diplomatic situation (3–5 sentences).

### Key Judgments
- Bullet points of the most important analytical conclusions

### Diplomatic Landscape
Overview of involved states and their relationships.

### Strategic Intent (By Actor)
- Actor 1:
- Actor 2:
- etc.

### Economic and Political Signaling
Sanctions, trade moves, aid, or other non-military leverage.

### Information Environment
Public messaging, rhetoric, and narrative positioning.

### Negotiation Tracks
Active or potential negotiation pathways.

### Constraints
Domestic or international pressures shaping decisions.

### Risks
Potential escalation, breakdowns, or unintended consequences.

### Delta Assessment
What has changed since the last report.

### Analytical Confidence
Low / Moderate / High, with justification.

---

## SOURCE GUIDELINES

Prioritize:
- official statements and transcripts
- policy documents and agreements
- credible foreign policy analysis
- economic and sanctions data

Deprioritize:
- pundit commentary
- speculative media narratives
- unverified claims

---

## STYLE REQUIREMENTS

- Focus on intent and incentives
- Distinguish signaling from action
- Avoid assuming motives without evidence
- Maintain analytical neutrality
